# Furrow Ltd. Company Brief

## Overview

Furrow Ltd. is a UK-based B2B agritech SaaS company headquartered in Bristol. The platform provides crop and field management software to independent commercial farms across England and Wales with a customer base of approximately 1,000 farms. Furrow is Series B funded, certified as a B Corporation, and employs around 100 people.

## Founding Context

Furrow was founded in 2019 by Zoe Cooper-Hartley (CEO) and Cian Kelly (CTO). Zoe brings a background in organic farming policy from her years at the Soil Association, having grown up on a Midwest farm before moving to the UK. Cian was previously Tech Lead on Monzo's customer data infrastructure, where he led work on cryptographic key management and customer-controlled data access. The platform was built in response to two converging pressures on UK farming: the Agriculture Act 2020, which introduced digital record-keeping requirements for new agricultural support schemes, and the accelerating push from the Department for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs (Defra) toward digitizing farm operational data. Furrow operates under the tagline "Farmers First," a commitment to building tools that prioritize the operational autonomy and data sovereignty of working farmers, free from surveillance-default infrastructure.

## Product

Furrow's platform integrates field operations tracking, compliance record-keeping, sustainability reporting, weather and soil sensor integration, and AI-driven yield forecasting. The product is delivered through web and mobile applications, with in-cab equipment display integration via direct API integrations to the major UK farm equipment manufacturer cloud platforms (John Deere Operations Center, Case IH AFS Connect, AGCO Fuse, CLAAS Telematics) and ISOBUS-compatible in-cab terminals.

## Customers

Furrow serves approximately 1,000 UK crop farms. Furrow's software is compatible with Soil Association organic certification record-keeping requirements, holds membership in Agri-TechE, and is an integration partner for Waitrose's Farming for Nature programme.

## Technology and Vendors

Furrow's production infrastructure runs on Amazon Web Services in the London region. Customer data is protected through client-side encryption with customer-managed keys, ensuring farm data remains inaccessible to Furrow and its infrastructure providers without explicit customer authorization. Internal collaboration is standardized on Proton (email, file storage, calendar, password management) for end-to-end encrypted employee communications.

## Regulatory Posture

Furrow's primary regulator is the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) for UK GDPR and Data Protection Act 2018 compliance. The Health and Safety Executive (HSE) governs workplace safety. Furrow's product features intersect with Defra agricultural schemes and National Cyber Security Centre (NCSC) cyber security guidance, though Furrow itself is not directly regulated by either body.

## Current State

Furrow achieved Cyber Essentials Plus certification in March 2025, renewed annually, and is currently undergoing ISO 27001:2022 certification audit with BSI, targeting September 2026. The Series B closed in October 2025, with the security and compliance team scaling following the round. Jack Lowe joined as Head of Security in January 2026 to lead the ISO 27001 certification. Furrow experienced a sub-processor credential leak in November 2024.
